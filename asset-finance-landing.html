Tax Advantages

The ATO is
practically
subsidising
your fleet.

Australia's tax framework around business assets is genuinely generous — if you structure your finance correctly. Most business owners leave serious money on the table.

📝

Instant Asset Write-Off

Eligible businesses can immediately deduct the full cost of qualifying assets in the year of purchase. Check current ATO thresholds with your accountant.

🔻

Depreciation Deductions

Assets financed through chattel mortgage or hire purchase allow you to claim depreciation year-on-year, reducing your taxable income consistently.

💸

GST Input Tax Credits

Registered businesses can claim the GST component on many financed assets upfront — effectively reducing the real cost of the asset from day one.

📌

Interest as a Business Expense

Finance charges on business assets are generally tax-deductible. The cost of borrowing is partially offset by the reduction in your tax liability.

FAQ

Straight answers.
No runaround.

What's the difference between chattel mortgage, hire purchase, and a finance lease?

+
A chattel mortgage means you own the asset from day one and the lender holds the asset as security — great for businesses that want to claim depreciation and GST immediately. Hire purchase means you make payments and ownership transfers at the end. A finance lease keeps the asset off your balance sheet and can have different tax treatments. We advise which structure best matches your tax position and business goals.

Can I finance used or second-hand equipment?

+
Yes. Many of our lending partners finance used assets — vehicles, plant and equipment, machinery — though the age, condition, and type of asset will affect the lending criteria and terms. Specialist lenders on our panel deal specifically in used heavy equipment and commercial vehicles. We'll match you to the right lender for the asset you have in mind.

How does asset finance affect my business's tax position?

+
Depending on the structure, you can claim depreciation on the asset, deduct finance charges as a business expense, claim GST input tax credits upfront (chattel mortgage), and potentially access Instant Asset Write-Off provisions. The exact tax outcome depends on your business structure, income, and applicable legislation — always confirm with your accountant. We'll help you understand the options so you're having an informed conversation with them.

What if my business is relatively new or has limited trading history?

+
Several lenders on our panel specifically cater to newer businesses, including low-doc and no-doc options for certain asset types. Strong personal credit, a deposit, or a clear business case can all help. It's always worth having the conversation — we've seen approvals for businesses in their first 12 months when the asset and circumstances are right.

How quickly can I actually have the asset?

+
Many asset finance approvals happen within 24–48 hours. Once approved, it typically takes a few more days for funds to be settled and the asset delivered or collected. Timing depends on the lender, asset type, and whether the supplier is ready. We push for speed because we know your business doesn't wait around.

Can you finance a whole fleet at once, or is it one vehicle at a time?

+
Both. We work with businesses acquiring a single vehicle and those building fleets of 20+. Fleet facilities can be structured to give you a pre-approved limit so you can add vehicles within that limit without a full re-assessment every time. This makes scaling particularly fast and efficient for high-growth operators.

Is there a minimum or maximum amount I can finance?

+
Minimums typically start around $5,000–$10,000 for most asset types. There's effectively no maximum — we've structured deals in the millions for large plant and equipment. For high-value or complex assets, the process involves slightly more documentation, but the principle is the same: we find the right lender for your specific situation.

You get access to expert advice, market comparison across 90+ lenders, and end-to-end application management at zero cost to your business. The discovery call is free.
